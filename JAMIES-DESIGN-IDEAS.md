## Typography / Fonts

Jamie likes **bold, editorial display/serif fonts** for headers. She shared these as references:

- **Filk** — bold serif display (on peach/coral background)
- **Quirk** — modern display font
- **Wild Mango** — playful bold serif
- **Fresh Lemonade** — handwritten/casual style
- **Wildkin** — bold display
- **Eighties Comeback** — retro bold serif, "A Beautifully Retrospective Typeface"

For body text she wants **clean, readable sans-serif** (referenced the black font on Sprinter's website — modern, bold, clean).

**Her exact words:**
- "I like these fonts"
- "I like the black font they use on her website" (Sprinter)

**Takeaway:** Headers should feel editorial/magazine-like with bold character. Body text should be clean system sans-serif.

## Language & Tone

Jamie wants the app copy to feel **warm, conversational, and approachable** — like talking to a friend, NOT a medical textbook.

**Her exact words:**
- "I want the language in the app to be comfortable and friendly. Like Hey babe,"
- "I also wanted the stack explanations to be science based and progressional but then have 'break it down' to simpler terms."
- "Like kinda more bro style talking but not all the way. Easier language like 'gain muscle, lean out, energized' etc."
- "Like a button that literally says dumb it down and ai versions simplifies it to a really mainstream understanding."

**Takeaway:** 
- Peptide content starts science-based, then has a "Break it down" or "Dumb it down" button that uses AI to simplify
- Copy throughout the app should be casual and friendly
- Use simple fitness language: "gain muscle", "lean out", "energized", "recover faster"

**Her exact words:**
- "I like buttons like this, kinda like my website actually."
- "Neutral vibes again"

## Peptide Section — Specific Ideas

Jamie wants the peptide/stacks section to feel like flipping through a **magazine** with editorial-style cards that make people want to explore.

**Her exact words:**
- "I really want under the peptide side there to be images that make people want to click and read about stacks."
- "Like this but have the girls face (ai) and the words say 'Peptide beauty'"
- "Or beauty & reverse aging"

**Takeaway:** 
- Use lifestyle/editorial images (AI-generated faces OK)
- Bold magazine-style headlines on cards
- Image + bold headline + short hook text
- Think magazine cover cards, NOT clinical data sheets

## Summary

Think: **Sprinter website meets health magazine**

- White/clean base with warm sunset color pops
- Editorial typography for headers
- Conversational, friendly tone
- Magazine-style cards for peptide content
- Minimal, premium, not tacky
- Different accent palettes for men vs women, same white base
- Rounded neutral buttons
- Clean bottom tab navigation
- Search bar at top for quick access
